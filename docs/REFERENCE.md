# REFERENCE — Design intent

![Reference cabin](../assets/reference/01-reference-cabin.png)

## What the reference image fixes

- **Form**: A-frame with steep roof slope (≈ 65°), enclosed cabin + front terrace on a single elevated platform.
- **Materials palette**:
  - **Roof**: matte black metal (standing seam or trapezoidal).
  - **Structural steel**: matte black powder-coat or epoxy finish.
  - **Wood**: warm honey-toned treated wood for cladding, ceiling lining, deck boards, and gable trim.
  - **Glass**: large-format tempered/laminated panels in slim black aluminum frames, floor-to-ceiling on the front gable.
- **Lighting**: warm-white interior, soft string lights on terrace, no harsh white floods.
- **Railing**: horizontal cable rails with wood top handrail.
- **Chimney**: black metal flue exiting the rear roof slope.

## Critical decisions for the structural engineer

These are pinned now so the engineer optimizes for them rather than re-deriving:

1. **The platform must clear the rocks** — no concrete pads between steel and rock. The columns connect plate-to-rock via chemical anchors only.
2. **Column heights are independent** — each of the 9 columns is sized to match its rock's actual height, ranging from ~0.6 m to ~1.8 m. The platform is level; the rocks are not.
3. **Front gable is glass-dominant** — the entire 18.6 m² front triangle is glazed minus a slim wood trim band at the base. The engineer must account for glass dead load + wind suction on this surface.
4. **Roof slope ≈ 65°** — steep enough to shed water and hail aggressively. Eaves overhang the platform by ~0.3 m on each side.
5. **Rafter length 6.89 m** — exceeds standard 6 m stock. Engineered splice or custom-length stock both acceptable; agent of record decides.
6. **Lateral bracing assumed in floor plane + diagonals between columns** — until the engineer says otherwise, both are budgeted in the BOM.
7. **Chimney exits rear slope, not ridge** — preserves the clean front-gable glass surface. Roof penetration flashing required.
